International Women's Day  ·  A Letter for Every Woman
Dear Woman,
This letter was written for you.
✦

There is a girl in your story

who learned early that love is a kind of labour —

that to show up for others

is to be handed a quiet crown

no one warns you about.

She carried it well.

Even when she didn't know she was carrying it.

✦   ✦   ✦

Then came the moment she left the familiar —

the city, the comfort, the version of herself

she already knew how to be.

She arrived somewhere new

with a bag full of hope

and a heart full of questions

she hadn't yet learned to ask out loud.

Independence, it turns out,

is not a door you walk through once.

It is a hallway.

And some mornings, it is very long.

✦   ✦   ✦
And then there was the hardest week.

You remember it.

The weight of it sitting in your chest

like a stone that hadn't decided

whether to sink or stay.

The deadline breathing.

The voice inside asking —

Who do you think you are
to be here?

But you stayed.

You did not leave quietly.

You did not leave at all.

And that is the thing about grit —

it is almost never loud.

It does not announce itself.

It is just you,

at an unglamorous hour,

choosing to continue.

✦   ✦   ✦

Then you began to speak.

Not because the fear left —

but because you decided

your voice mattered more

than your silence.

Step by step.

Risk by quiet risk.

Word by careful word.

And the world looked back and said:

we see you.

Not because you were perfect —

but because you were consistent.

And consistency, it turns out,

is its own kind of courage.

✦   ✦   ✦
So here is what I need you to remember:

You were never behind.

You were building foundation

while others were building noise.

The late nights matter.

The invisible effort matters.

The version of you who almost gave up

and didn't —

she matters most of all.

Keep betting on your curiosity.

It is one of your greatest gifts.

Not because it always leads to answers —

but because it refuses to stop asking questions.

And do not shrink your voice

just because you are still learning.

The people shaping the future

are not the ones who know everything.

They are the ones willing to begin

anyway.

✦   ✦   ✦

You are a woman who understands

that building something that matters

is not optional where you come from.

It is survival.

It is love.

It is the future you are choosing

to shape with your own hands.

One day you will walk into a room

where decisions about the future are being made

and you will not hesitate at the door.

You will not ask for permission

to take up space.

You will simply enter.

And something in the room will shift.

Because that is what you do.

You shift things.

Quietly.

Persistently.

Powerfully.

✦   ✦   ✦

The woman who survived her hardest season

will build things that outlive her.

Younger women will look at her life

and think:

If she did it —
maybe I can too.

She will.

She already has.

She is.

With love, pride, and unwavering belief —
Habiba Musa (Baby)
International Women's Day · March 2025

This letter was born from one woman's story — and dedicated to every woman who has ever carried a quiet crown, survived a hard week, and chosen to keep going.
